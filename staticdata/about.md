## About Me

#### Where it all began

I was born in a small rural town in eastern Colorado. In the early
days you wouldn't find me anywhere near a computer, unless it was my
cousin and I's N64. We spent many days outside on bikes, helping family
friends wrangling steers, or hunting and fishing with my uncle.
In high school you would find me helping build sets for the school play (sometimes even practicing lines for a part!)
or playing my tuba in the band room.


#### Changing my major

I graduated high school, not at the bottom of my class, but certainly not
at the top. I auditioned for and was accepted into the school of music, playing
the tuba. I loved college, especially that first year as a music major.



Still, I wanted more, I had a desire to learn more about the universe.
So, after that amazing year, I changed my major to physics. That set me
down a path of learning about the forces that govern our existence.
From free body diagrams to learning how quantum mechanics allows transistors
to work I tried my best to unravel the mysteries in front of me.



Unfortunately, as I came to the end of my tract and I started looking for jobs,
I faced a dilemma. To obtain a job in the astronomy field I felt more and more
like I needed to go to grad school. Now don't get me wrong, I loved school,
but I just was (and still am) not sure I could navigate through complexities
of grad school. Also, I didn't want to pay for it, haha.

#### Changing My Major Again

Boy, this seems familiar. Yes, once more I changed my major, kinda. With a semester
left in my physics tract I started another Bachelors in software engineering.
I had a goal of finishing the software engineering bachelors in just 4 semesters.
Just one small problem, I had no idea how to code. I had poked around in python a bit,
but I had no idea how applications were architected or built in any kind of responsible manner.
So slowly, and I mean slowly, I learned the in and outs of programming.
I ended up finishing (and leaving) school after 7 years. It was a long time, but
I would do it all again and not change a thing.
